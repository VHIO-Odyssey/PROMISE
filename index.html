PROMISE

Prognostic Score for Hospitalized Cancer Patients (PROMISE) Tool

The objective of this tool is to predict the probability of mortality at 90 days after admission in oncologic metastatic patients with active treatment and unscheduled admission. The data used was from the study PLANTOLOGY (NCT05534178 at https://clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/show/NCT05534178?term=plantology&draw=2&rank=1=) and approved by all the independent ethics committee at each center (Vall d'Hebron: PR(AG)92/2020).
All laboratory parameters (LDH, PMN, and albumin) were collected within 24-48 hours from admission.
The inclusion criteria were:
  • 1. Unplanned hospitalization
  • 2. Metastatic disease or unresectable
  • 3. On active treatment (last 6 months prior to admission).
  • 4. More than 18 years old.

About the tool
To address the lack of standardized prognostic tools for hospitalized oncology patients, we initiated an observational prospective study at Vall d’Hebron Hospital and Vall d’Hebron Institute of Oncology (VHIO) during 2020. Dr. Mirallas in collaboration with colleagues from the medical oncology department, nutritionists, and nurses, we gathered data from over 1,500 patients admitted to our ward at Vall d’Hebron University Hospital. One year later, our findings were validated in collaboration with two additional institutions in Barcelona—Hospital del Mar and Hospital de la Santa Creu i Sant Pau.
The Odyssey Data Science Group from Vall d’Hebron Institute of Oncology performed the statistical analysis, modeling, and developed the web tool for clinical application. Our results, published in The Lancet Regional Health – Europe, introduce the PROMISE tool, designed to provide objective prognostic information for clinicians managing hospitalized cancer patients with advanced disease not in exclusive palliative care. We anticipate that this tool will significantly enhance physicians' ability to optimize care for hospitalized cancer patients and support medical decisions.

Oriol Mirallas is a medical oncologist and clinical investigator specialized in Phase I Clinical Trials and Lung Cancer at the Vall d'Hebron Institute of Oncology (VHIO). Dr. Mirallas' research focuses on developing novel therapeutics for patients with cancer and prognostic tools essential for informed treatment decisions and patient care in advanced cancer.
“I realized during my second year of residency at Vall d’Hebron Hospital that prognosis for hospitalized oncologic patients lacked standardization, with many decisions made solely based on ECOG PS. I strongly believed that this approach needed improvement, leading to the initiation of Project PLANTOLOGY, which ultimately resulted in the creation of the PROMISE tool that I hope will help many patients and physicians”.
